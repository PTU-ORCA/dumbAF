Engineering Mathematics-II (BTAM201-23)

Course Objectives

The objective of Engineering Mathematics-II is to enable students to understand concepts like:

  • Elimination methods for solving linear systems of equations
  • Inverse of matrices
  • Vector spaces
  • Ordinary and partial differential equations

The goal is to equip students with the ability to apply these techniques to solve engineering problems.

Course Outcomes

  • Verify theoretical concepts through practical application.
  • Visualize and conceptualize engineering problems.
  • Mathematically model engineering problems using matrices, ODEs, and PDEs.
  • Draw conclusions from data and develop skills applicable to industrial problems.
  • Find solutions to engineering problems from an application-oriented perspective.

Detailed Syllabus

PART-A

Unit-I: System of Linear Equations

  • Rank of a matrix
  • Echelon form of matrix
  • Homogeneous and Non-homogeneous systems of linear equations
  • Consistency and inconsistency of systems of equations
  • Gauss elimination method
  • Inverse of a matrix
  • Gauss-Jordan method

Unit-II: Vector Spaces

  • Vector spaces and Subspaces
  • Linear independence and dependence of vectors
  • Dimension and basis
  • Linear transformation, rank and nullity theorem (without proof)
  • Matrix associated with Linear Transformation
  • Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
  • Cayley-Hamilton theorem
  • Algebraic and geometric multiplicity
  • Similar and diagonalizable matrices

PART-B

Unit-III: Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs)

  • Formation and solution of differential equations
  • Initial and boundary value problems
  • Solution methods:
    • Separable equations
    • Equations reducible to separable form
    • Exact differential equations
    • Integrating factors
    • Linear first-order equations
    • Bernoulli and Riccati equations
    • Clairaut's equation
    • Higher-order linear equations (constant & variable coefficients)
    • Variation of parameters
    • Undetermined coefficients
    • Finding particular integrals
  • Applications:
    • Electric RLC circuits
    • Beam deflection
    • Simple harmonic motion
    • Population decay models
    • Orthogonal trajectories

Unit-IV: Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)

  • Formation of first and second-order PDEs
  • Solution of first-order PDEs:
    • Lagrange's equation
    • Surfaces orthogonal to a given family
    • Nonlinear first-order equations
    • Charpit's method
  • Higher-order linear PDEs with constant coefficients
